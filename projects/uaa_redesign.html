The Problem:

The University of Alaska's website needs its page architecture restructured. Key information for prospective students are often obfuscated within many layers of link hierarchies. Our team aims to alleviate the stress of researching key components of the daily life as a UAA student.

The Solution:

We developed a balsamiq prototype reorganizing its main fixed header to be consistent across all pages, and focused on restructuring how a propective student wants to research meal plans and dining options.

My Role:

Prototyping, UX design, Research

Tools:

Pen and Paper, Balsamiq, GIMP, Photoshop

UAA Redesign

Discovery

First we do a comparative analysis through a handful of major universities looking at homepage navigation bars. We talley for most common header and subheader names. We can quickly explore each design and see how UAA can line up based on the type of university it is.

Research

We construct 5 persona types using interview data and develop numerous user scenarios our persona would likely go through when browsing the UAA website. We create an interaction cost for each one by rating how often a persona would do a scenario and measuring it against both Page distance and clicks + scrolls.

This lets us definitively measure the original UAA site navigation against our redesign

Design Decisions

One of the first choices we had to make is dealing with the split between the type of students we want to display in our header navigation.

When thinking about conjoining both prospective and current types within the larger “Student” second level page, our left navigation bar could become cluttered leading to slowdowns in finding specific topics.

The homepage primary labels are now always visible to the user for ease of access

Our left navibar allows for visible subheaders without obfuscation.

Prototype

Balsamiq let us easily pull graphics from UAA while maintaining quick low fidelity iteration. By converting to fullscreen pdfs, we better visualized our architecture witthout needing to focus on creating the functionality.
